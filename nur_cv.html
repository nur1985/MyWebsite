Postdoctoral Research Associate
Expert in Data acquisition, Detector Operations, and Neutrino Scattering

Qualifications Summary
In-depth experience in medium and high energy physics experiments with a variety of data acquisition and detector systems, their development and operations. A solid background in electron-nucleus and neutrino-nucleus physics. Vast experience in Monte Carlo simulations. A strong record of leading large scale projects in big collaborations in national lab environment.

MINERvA Experiment Contribution at FermilabMay 2015 - Now
A neutrino scattering experiment that seeks to measure low energy neutrino interactions both in support of neutrino oscillation experiments and to study the strong dynamics of nucleus that affect these interactions.
  • Serving as data acquisition (DAQ) system and detector expert to ensure continuous data taking and troubleshooting any DAQ related issues. Also configured, tested and installed new DAQ and online monitoring systems. Installed new firmwares for FPGA based Front End Boards (FEBs) and Chain Read Out Controllers with Ethernet interface (CROCEs) necessary for the medium energy high intensity neutrino run.
  • Currently, developing analysis infrastructure and analyzing data on antineutrino scattering on electrons to reduce MINERvA’s dominant systematic uncertainty from flux as a function of energy.
  • Architect of the University Remote Operation Center (UROC) infrastructure for MINERvA remote shift; Maintain and communicate as required for continued running of all 13 UROC stations all over the world.
  • Leading the MINERvA operations team of 10 Ph.D., 2 MS and 1 undergraduate student.
  • Developed tools to automate many online system, implemented checklist based shifts and eliminated evening, and owl shifts enabling the reduction of the required manpower by 2/3rd.
  • Created web infrastructure to get live status of the MINERvA DAQ, detector and other key control systems. One of the essential tools for the shifters and operations group.
  • Assembled prototype MINERvA detector module to setup two test stands with FEBs, Photomultiplier Tubes (PMTs), CROCEs, and DAQ and performed extensive bench tests of the newly developed firmwares for the high intensity medium energy data collection and calibration.
  • Served to takeover MINOS near detector operations and have been acting as the system administrator for the MINERvA and MINOS DAQs, online monitoring system, and test stands.

Q-weak Experiment Contribution at Jefferson LabAug 2010 - Apr 2015
First direct measurement of the weak charge of the proton through a high precision measurement of the parity-violating asymmetry in electron scattering that aims to test the predictions of the Standard Model.
  • Performed an independent analysis on first measurement of the transverse asymmetry in Δ resonance production, which gives novel access to the elastic form-factors of the Δ.
  • Implemented beam modulation (BMod) system, a necessary system for parity violating experiment to correct for false asymmetry, to characterize detector response with respect to the driven beam jitter.
  • Commissioned the BMod system (two pairs of dipoles and associated readout electronics, VME and NIM based DAQ system) using the simulated optics from OptiM and extensive bench tests and served as the system leader.
  • Extracted preliminary detector sensitivities and developed technique to track changes in the beamline optics such as dispersion at the target and beam position coupling from BMod system.
  • Designed a corrector magnet for the Q-weak Toroidal magnet (QTor) to improve background and implemented a real time magnetic field monitoring system for the QTor.
  • Built online analysis display software for real time data monitoring.
  • Served as Q-weak analysis coordinator and performed overall data quality checks to sort good and bad data based on various conditions also performed database stress test and analyzed part of the production data set.
  • Analyzed helicity correlated pedestals to improve false asymmetry contribution for the entire Q-weak data set.
Nuclear Transparency of Kaons at Jefferson LabAug 2007 - Jul 2010
This experiment found the first evidence of the novel phenomena known as color transparency in pions. The pion data also include some kaons, which were selected for this analysis. Extracted the kaon transparencies by comparing the electro-production of kaons from various nuclear targets to deuterium. Also extracted the effective kaon-nucleon, pion-nucleon and proton-nucleon cross sections from the transparencies and compared with world data.

Nuclear data structure and the decay scheme at NNDCJun 2005 - Jul 2007
Evaluated nuclear data structure and the decay scheme of the mass-chain A = 225 on the basis of available data to date, examined their different decay schemes, and analyzed them to update the database of the National Nuclear Data Center at Brookhaven National Laboratory.
